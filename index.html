E-mories

A distraction-free physical platform to remotely share personal emotions with close friends and family

What Is E-mories?

With E-mories you can record and send short messages with close ones. This can be people you can't get to see because of distance, friends or family you miss or simply just your bestie that you want to stay in touch with.


With the E-mories ball you can record a message to send. You can talk about anything from how your day was to how sick you are of the pandemic, to a beautiful butterfly you saw today, or a good memory from that time you went on a roadtrip together. The idea is that you can share whatever you want, preferrably something with a positive vibe, but it can also be venting, or anything in between.


After recording, you can choose a colour that goes with your message, whichever colour you think is suitable. When you've found your desired colour and are happy with your message, its time to send! The ball you are connected with (your friend, a family member or similar) will then light up in the colour you seleceted, and they can choose to listen to your message!

Technical Details

The prototype is built up of different sensors and a christmas decoration ball used as a shell. The shell is lightly polished with sandpaper to reduce the visibility of what is inside. Other than this the prototype is built up of:

  • An ESP32. This is the microcontroller keeping everything working
  • A NeoPixel LED strip. This displays the colours and lights on the prototype
  • An accelerometer and gyroscope. This helps calculating the rotation of the ball.
  • A vibration motor. This gives the haptic feedback when the colour is selected
  • Jumper cables. These help wiring connections together.
  • Flex sensor. This detects when the user sqeezes the ball


I'm using a library called MPU6050_tocken (can be found here) to calculate the angle values of the accelerometer. Then, each value (z, y and z) are mapped into RGB-values. My full code can be found on github here, and the team code combined can be found here.

The Design process

The initial concept the team presented was completely different from the one we proceeded with. But, after the new idea had been settled, I was focusing on the colour selection. This was suggested to be a shake with a random colour selection, mimicing the interaction of a dice.


With the shake I went through different stages of finding a set of colours that could be representative for the most used colours, using a diary study. However, I then figured out that it might be desireable to adjust the colour brightness or saturation once the correct hue was selected. I did informal chats with friends and teaching staff to figure out which way would be the best, and got some feedback from the team appraisals that were a part of the course.


Ulimately, I ended with rotation of the ball to select colour to be the most suitable way. I completely scrapped the shake interaction, because this was perceived from the users as not being in control and might be a tedeous process. The new rotation allowed for the users to find a colour they were happy with themselves, and then adjust the colour further. I chose to incorporate a squeeze as a way of locking down the colour to tie better with the interaction forms used by the other users.

Project Outcomes - Was The Project Successful?

Whether the project was successful or not depends on which angle you want to view it from.

Did we manage to create a prototype that demonstrates the intention of the project?
Yes. The balls we have individually created demonstates the intended interaction and works for the users to get an idea of the concept.

Did I manage to create an interaction metaphor for selecting a colour that make sense for the users?
Possibly. I tested and refined the concept several times, and believe I have come up with something that makes more sense to the users than the original idea. However, the execution of this interaction is somewhat unstable and and the sensitivity might not be just right.

Did we as a team manage to create and test a full experience of the whole concept?
No. Putting together the pieces was more time consuming than anticipated and we were never able to test the experience as a whole. There are potential gaps in the full experience that we never thought of as individuals working with different sections.
